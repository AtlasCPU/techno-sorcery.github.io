Techno-Sorcery

"Man is still the most extraordinary computer of all."


A friend and I spent a month-and-a-half practically re-doing the MESA Machine for the regional competition. Here's what we came up with.

MESA Machine

After winning first place in our school's preliminary MESA Machine competition, my group decided to kick things up even further.

Description


A metal ball bearing starts on the ramp at the top of the machine, where it has the most gravitational potential energy. When a cord connectecd to a duct tape door is yanked, the ball bearing is released. Its motion is translated to kinetic energy as it continues down both ramps, and into a small series of PVC pipes.

The bearing rolls down a final ramp before falling on the first mousetrap. When triggered, the kill bar flies foward and releases its hold on the previously taut drawbridge string, allowing it to fall. This creates an incline for a couple small marbles to roll down and into the funnel, where then are directed onto a second mouse trap.

When triggered, the second mouse trap yanks back a small door blocking the movement of a cork ball, allowing it to roll down the final ramp and into the pulley bucket. The weighed-down bucket falls onto the switch, triggering the fan.
